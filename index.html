About

Mithila Folk Art

Our Mission
Mithila Folk Art, also known as Madhubani painting, is a centuries-old artistic tradition originating from the Mithila region of Bihar, India. This vibrant art form, traditionally practiced by women, uses natural dyes and intricate patterns to depict mythological themes, nature, and everyday life. Each painting is more than just colors on canvasit's a narrative that reflects the cultural ethos, social values, and spiritual beliefs of the community.

Our Mission
At Mithila Folk Art, we are committed to preserving and promoting this cultural heritage by creating timeless pieces that resonate with both traditional and contemporary art enthusiasts. Our artworks are crafted with meticulous attention to detail, ensuring each piece carries the authenticity and soul of Mithila tradition.

The Artist Behind the Art
Rashmi Dhar, the creative force behind our artworks, transitioned from an engineering career to pursue her passion for art. Her fascination with Mithila Folk Art began in 2020, and she honed her skills through extensive training under renowned artists at the National Institute of Mithila Art. Rashmis work blends classic motifs with modern aesthetics, making the art form accessible to a global audience.

Why Choose Mithila Folk Art?
Authentic Craftsmanship: Every artwork is hand-painted using traditional techniques passed down through generations.
Eco-Friendly Approach: We use natural dyes and sustainable materials to honor the earth-friendly practices of the art form.
Customization Available: We create bespoke pieces tailored to your space and style.
Cultural Legacy: Owning a piece of Mithila Folk Art means becoming part of a rich cultural history.

How is Rashmi's Art

Rashmi's art is a vivid reflection of creativity, emotion, and cultural richness. Her work captivates the viewer through its intricate details, bold colors, and expressive forms. Each piece tells a story, inviting the audience into a world where imagination and reality merge seamlessly.

Beyond aesthetics, Rashmi's art often conveys meaningful messages. Her pieces frequently explore themes of identity, community, and the interconnectedness of life. By weaving elements of her cultural heritage with contemporary influences, she creates art that resonates with diverse audiences.
